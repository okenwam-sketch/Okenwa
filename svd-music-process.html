Piano first. Always.
Start here
Write the emotion in one sentence
What is this song about? What does it feel like? This anchors every decision that follows.
MIDI piano → Ableton → Spitfire LABS piano

Find 3-4 chords that match the feeling
Minor 7ths, major 9ths, suspended chords — these are RnB flavours. Play until something gives you a feeling in your chest. That's the one.
Stay in one key. If unsure — play a chord you like, then only use notes that sound good with it. That's diatonic playing and it always works.
Loop and just listen for 2 minutes
Before writing a single word, let the chords play. Melodies and words start coming naturally. Don't force it.

3 — Write to the music
Let the chords tell you what to say.
Most important
Loop the track and hum — don't write yet
Find the hook melody first by humming over the chords. The chorus melody is the heart — everything else builds around it.
Write lyrics to what you're already humming
Melody came first — now fit words to that melody. Not the other way round. This is how every great RnB song gets written.
Write fast and messy first. The first draft captures the truth of the feeling. Perfection kills the first draft.
Structure: hook → verse → bridge
Write chorus/hook first. Verses lead into it. Bridge last — the emotional turn where the song shifts perspective.

Now make it professional.
Build

Proper bass line + sidechain
Add passing notes between chord changes. Set up sidechain compression — bass ducks when kick hits. This one technique closes most of the gap between amateur and professional low end.

6 — Mix the beat
Clean, balanced, translates anywhere.
Mix
Gain staging first
Every track peaks -12 to -6dB before any plugins. This is the foundation. Most beginners skip it and wonder why the mix sounds crushed.
EQ every track — cut before boost
High-pass all non-bass instruments below 80Hz. Cut 200-400Hz mud where needed. Each element gets its own frequency space.
Ableton EQ EightiZotope
Pan for width, check in mono
Kick/snare/bass → centre. Pads/harmonies → ±40-70%. Check mono every 10 mins. If something disappears in mono it only exists in stereo — that's a problem on clubs and phones.
The car test
Phone speaker + ESI monitor + earphones. Sounds good on all three = the mix works.

7 — Final vocal recording
On the real beat. Full performance.
The real take
Re-record everything fresh
Don't keep demo vocals. 3-5 takes per section minimum. Your timing and energy lock into the real beat naturally.
Double-track the chorus
Record chorus lead twice, pan ±15-20%. Makes the chorus feel wider and more powerful. Free and one of the most effective professional tricks in RnB.
Stack harmonies and ad-libs
3-part harmony on chorus. Whisper ad-libs, high falsetto, low backing tone — each on its own track for individual control.

RnB drum basics
The only drum knowledge you need · Week 1-2
Kick on 1 and 3. Snare on 2 and 4. Hi-hat every 8th note. This is every RnB song ever made.
Add swing: right-click MIDI clip → Groove → 8B Swing. Makes drums feel human instead of robotic.
Ghost notes: quiet snare hits between main beats at 30-50% velocity. Adds groove texture.
Don't use pre-made loops for the first 2 weeks. Program by hand. Your ear learns faster.
Study 3 RnB songs you love — listen ONLY to the drums and rebuild each pattern from scratch.
Kick & bass relationship
Biggest gap between amateur and pro · Week 3-4
Where the kick hits the bass should be silent or very short. They fight for the same frequency space.
Sidechain compression: compressor on bass, sidechain input = kick. Bass ducks every time kick hits. This is what makes RnB feel punchy and professional.
Check in mono — if the low end gets muddy, kick and bass are clashing. Fix timing before EQ.
Kick EQ: boost 60-80Hz (thump), cut 300-400Hz (mud), boost 3-5kHz (click).
Bass EQ: high-pass below 35Hz, boost 80-100Hz warmth, cut 200-300Hz mud.
RnB chord flavours
Your piano lessons directly feed this
Minor 7ths (Cmin7 = C Eb G Bb) — melancholy, introspective. Most common in RnB.
Major 9ths (Fmaj9 = F A C E G) — warm, hopeful, dreamy. SZA energy.
Suspended 2nds (Gsus2 = G A D) — unresolved tension. Perfect for pre-choruses.
